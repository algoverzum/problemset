## Seeds
I have one kapripa seed. If I plant the seed, a kapripa plant will grow and within one year it will bring exactly one fruit that has 3 seeds inside, after which it will die. Next year, I will plant all 3 seeds, and the 3 kapripa plants will produce 3 seeds each, so I will have 9 seeds in total at the end of the second year. In the third year, I will again plant all of them and collect all the seeds at the end of the year, and so on. I will stop when I have at least $N$ kapripa seeds. How many seeds will I have at that point?

### Input
The first line of the input contains $N$ - the desired number of kapripa seeds.

### Output
Print a single integer number, the number of seeds at the end of the process.

### Constraints
* $1 \le N \le 100\,000$

### Example input
    80

### Example output
    81

### Explanation of the example
After 4 years we will have 81 seeds and that is enough, since we want at least 80.
