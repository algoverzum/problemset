## Smallest Divisor
I will give you a number $N$ that is at least 2. Can you tell its smallest divisor, which is greater than 1?

### Input
The input contains a single positive integer $N$. 

### Output
Print a single number, the smallest divisor of $N$ that is greater than 1. There is always at least one such divisor of $N$, the number itself.

### Constraints
* $2 \le N \le 100\,000$

### Example input
    77

### Example output
    7

### Explanation of the example
The divisors of 63 are 1, 7, 11 and 77. The smallest of them that is bigger than 1 is the 7.
